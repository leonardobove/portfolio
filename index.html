Who I Am

About Me

leonardo.bove01@gmail.com leonardobove leonardobove

I'm a Graduate Research Fellow at the University of Pisa, working at the intersection of quantum physics and electronic engineering. My research focuses on measuring relaxation and decoherence times in superconducting qubits — building the precision instrumentation that makes quantum computing possible.

In summer 2025, I joined Fermilab's Superconducting Quantum Materials and Systems (SQMS) center in Batavia, IL, where I designed the QPCB (Quantum Pulse Conversion Board) and engineered Qubase, a Python pulse sequencer for QICK FPGA systems. That same year, I was invited to present my research at the ETH Zürich Quantum Device Lab.

Beyond research, I served as CTO of E-Team Squadra Corse (Formula Student EV), played violin for 12 years, and earned 3 merit scholarships from the Cantiere Internazionale d'Arte di Montepulciano.
