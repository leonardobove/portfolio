About

I am a Graduate Research Fellow in the Department of Information Engineering at the University of Pisa, where I design and operate instrumentation for superconducting qubit experiments.

My work lives at the intersection of quantum physics and engineering: I build the classical control systems — FPGA firmware, microwave electronics, readout chains — that make quantum processors measurable and controllable. My master's thesis, developed partly at Fermilab SQMS, focused on FPGA-based qubit readout using the QICK platform, and on Qubase, a Python pulse sequencer I engineered for high-throughput qubit characterization.

I have performed the first qubit measurements ever conducted in the Engineering Department of the University of Pisa, graduating with 110/110 summa cum laude (GPA 4.0/4.0) at both bachelor's and master's level. Beyond research, I have led 25+ engineers as CTO of the University of Pisa FSAE electric racing team, and I am an active tutor and mentor for younger students.

Research Interests

Superconducting qubit characterization & control  ·  FPGA-based quantum control systems  ·  Microwave engineering & low-noise readout chains  ·  Embedded systems & real-time firmware  ·  Quantum error characterization (T₁, T₂)

Dispersive Readout Hamiltonian

$$\hat{H}_{\text{disp}} = \hbar\!\left(\omega_r + \chi\hat{\sigma}_z\right)\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a}$$

where $\chi$ is the dispersive coupling strength and $\hat{\sigma}_z$ is the qubit Pauli operator. The state-dependent shift of the resonator frequency enables quantum non-demolition readout.
